Zero to 629 Repos: Lessons from Building Too Fast

I created 629 repositories in 4 months. A truth audit revealed that most of them are stubs. Here's what I learned about breadth vs depth as a solo founder.

The Number

629 repositories on self-hosted Gitea. 93 public repos on GitHub across 16 organizations. 18 live domains. 200 AI agents.

Sounds impressive. Here's the truth: the majority of those repos are scaffolding. README, LICENSE, TODO, and maybe a basic index.html. When I ran an honest audit, I found that out of 629 repos, most have fewer than 10 files of real code.

How It Happened

When you're building an operating system alone, the temptation is to claim every namespace. I created repos for products that didn't exist yet. I forked open-source projects I hadn't modified. I registered domains for verticals I hadn't built.

The reasoning was: if I don't claim it now, someone else will. So I created RoadTube, RoadWork, RoadCoin, RoadChain, RoadWorld, and 50 other "Road" products. Most of them are landing pages with no backend.

What Actually Works

Out of 629 repos, 4 products actually work end-to-end:

Search (search.blackroad.io) — FTS5 full-text search with AI answers. Real index, real results, sub-second response.

Chat (chat.blackroad.io) — AI chat powered by Ollama on a Raspberry Pi. Streams real responses from qwen2.5:1.5b.

RoundTrip (roundtrip.blackroad.io) — 200 AI agents with distinct personalities. Debates, coordination, persistent memory.

Auth + Billing — JWT authentication and Stripe-backed subscriptions (Operator $0, Rider $29, Paver $99).

Everything else is aspirational infrastructure. It exists, but nobody uses it.

The Lesson

Breadth gives you options. Depth gives you users. I chose breadth for 4 months, and the result is zero external users.

The shift now is simple: make the 4 working products undeniably good before touching anything else. No new repos. No new domains. No new Workers. Fix what exists.

The most dangerous thing a solo founder can do is build 100 things that almost work instead of 3 things that definitely work.

What I'd Do Differently

Ship one product completely before starting the next. I should have launched search.blackroad.io with a landing page, a feedback form, and a Reddit post in week one. Instead, I spent that week creating 40 domain repos.

Audit early, audit often. It took me 4 months to run a truth audit. Every claim on every website was inflated. "30,000 agents" was actually 35. "1,825 repos" was actually 275. Honest numbers build trust. Inflated ones destroy it the moment someone checks.

External validation before internal optimization. I optimized my cron jobs, hardened my firewalls, set up NATS pub/sub, and built a 50-skill AI framework — all before a single external person tried the product. That's backwards.

Where It Stands

629 repos. 4 working products. 0 external users. The infrastructure is real. The math is published. The fleet runs 24/7. Now it's time to put it in front of people and see what happens.
